# Use Cases

## Overview

Proof of Contribution may support several ecosystem functions if implemented with careful safeguards. These use cases should be treated as research directions, not guaranteed protocol outcomes.

## Contributor History

A contributor profile could preserve a history of verified work across documentation, code, governance, research, and infrastructure. This may help communities understand who has contributed meaningfully over time.

## Governance Context

Governance participants may benefit from contribution-aware context. For example, proposal reviewers may inspect whether a contributor has relevant history in infrastructure, documentation, or research. Contribution records should inform discussion, not automatically replace governance rights.

## Grants and Funding Review

Contribution history may help evaluate grant applicants or ecosystem funding requests. A verified record of prior useful work can reduce information asymmetry. However, contribution history should not become an exclusionary gate that prevents new contributors from participating.

## Maintainer and Reviewer Selection

Communities may use contribution records to identify reviewers for documentation, code, translation, governance, or infrastructure topics. Category-specific reputation is important because expertise is not universal.

## Ecosystem Memory

Proof of Contribution can preserve historical knowledge about who built tools, wrote guides, solved onboarding problems, operated infrastructure, or contributed to governance. This helps prevent ecosystem memory from disappearing into temporary chats or short-lived campaigns.

## Anti-Spam and Quality Filtering

Contribution attestations may help distinguish durable work from low-effort activity. However, this must be done carefully to avoid centralizing judgment or over-penalizing new participants.

## Identity Without Full Disclosure

A contributor may want to build reputation under a pseudonym without exposing personal identity. A Proof of Contribution framework should allow reputation to attach to a persistent contributor identity without requiring unnecessary personal data.

## Research and Ecosystem Analytics

Aggregated contribution records may help researchers study ecosystem growth, contributor retention, documentation maturity, governance participation, and infrastructure diversity.

## Use Case Limitations

Proof of Contribution should not be used as:

- A guaranteed reward system
- A replacement for governance deliberation
- A universal contributor ranking
- A financial asset
- A centralized control mechanism
- A tool for excluding new contributors

## Research Position

The strongest use cases are those that preserve memory, improve context, and support accountable participation without converting contribution into a farmable metric.
